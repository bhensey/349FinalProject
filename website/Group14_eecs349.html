Lost Redditors 

Course: eecs 349 
Instructor: Doug Downey 

Group Members: Bennett Hensey, Jason Liu, Michael Threatt

Task: Our task was to create a content classifier for Reddit for moderator purposes. Reddit moderators have the difficult task of scrubbing irrelevant content from subreddits. This is a daunting task since there are thousands of subreddits to moderate. We aim to use the machine learning concepts learned in class to create a tool to assist in that task. 

Approach: We used the Reddit API along with NLTK in python to acquire our data sets.Our data consisted of the number of comments, reddit score, word frequency of the titles. We used both bayes net, J48 ,neural networks to see which was the most effective implementation. 

Key results: Bayes net offered slightly better validation accuracy compared to the neural network. This isn't particulary suprising. This may change if a larger data set is used or the number of hidden layers changes. The Neural net validation and training accuracy are shown below and were compared to J48 descision trees and Bayes net in the final report. 


We trained machine learning models that classify subreddits based on title strings using a bayes net, J48 decision tree, and tensorflow keras neural net with accuracies of 84.4%, 78.5%, and 85.5% respectively. While neural nets presented higher accuracy, we believe Bayes classifiers are better for their interpretability. We tested our bayes net model using posts taken from different time periods to determine the generalizability of our model. Our all-time top posts model achieved accuracies of 78.5%, 83.0%, and 85.1% with datasets of top posts from the previous week, month, and year. Performing stemming with our bag-of-words approach improved accuracy, but we expect that we higher accuracies will come from more complex feature selection (sentence structure, grammar, valence). In the future, we could try increase our accuracy by using word embeddings.
